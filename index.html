Emphasizing Interpersonal Connection in Seminar-Style Blended Learning Courses

Research Question

How can the Higher Education Administration program at Northwestern University maintain or strengthen the high quality, interpersonal learning experiences of graduate-level seminar courses when offering them in a blended learning format?

Background/Context

Four blended format courses were examined over the course of one year from Spring Quarter 2016 through Winter Quarter 2017. Changes were made to courses and the blended format over the course of the year based on feedback from faculty and students.

Literature Review

  1. Blended Learning Models and Practices in Higher Education. Definition and uses of blended learning.
  2. Differences in Online and In-Person Teaching and Learning. Educational technology tools and strategies.
  3. Interaction and Engagment within Online and Blended Learning. Community of Inquiry Theoretical Framework (Garrison, 2003, 2017).

Data Collection Methods

  1. Artifact Analyis. An examination syllabi and learning management system course sites of blended format courses.
  2. Student Surveys. Two surveys of students at the end of each blended format course.
  3. Faculty Inteviews. Interviews with faculty after the conclusion of each blended format course.
